# **Mastering Whole Wheat Sourdough: My Go-To Recipe & Why It's a Game-Changer**

There's something profoundly grounding about the rhythm of sourdough baking—the soft glug of an active starter, the dough's gentle rise under a tea towel, the oven's anticipatory heat. Recently, I made this [**Whole Wheat Sourdough Bread**](https://www.farmhouseonboone.com/how-to-make-whole-wheat-sourdough-bread/) from Farmhouse on Boone with fresh milled flour, and it quickly became my family's staple. Hearty, nutty, and robust, it's the kind of loaf that pairs perfectly with organic soups or slathered in grass-fed butter for kiddo snack time.​

At **Mama Fern**, where we prioritize organic fabrics and faith-rooted designs for little ones, this recipe feels like an extension of that philosophy: simple ingredients, natural processes, and nourishment that lasts. In this post, I'll walk you through the **exact steps**, plus why sourdough—especially whole wheat—is worth mastering for health, ease, and everyday joy.&#x20;

Prep time: 15 mins. Bake: 36 mins. Ferment: 16 hrs. Total: \~50 mins active. Yields 1 boule, 12 servings.​&#x20;

Or be like us and ALWAYS double your recipes!

## **Ingredients (Straight from Lisa at Farmhouse on Boone- a gem!)**

This is a **no-fuss, whole-food list**—just four staples for maximum flavor:

* 475 g **freshly milled whole wheat flour** (I love sourcing organic and/or local direct from the farmer!).​
* 325 g **water** (filtered, room temp to encourage fermentation).
* 100 g **active sourdough starter** (fed roughly 4-12 hours prior, bubbly and peaked preferred).
* 10 g **sea salt** (fine for even distribution).

**Nutrition per slice** (approx.): 142 kcal, 30g carbs, 5g protein, 4g fiber, minimal sugar.&#x20;

## **Step-by-Step: Baking Your Whole Wheat Sourdough Boule**

Follow these precisely as in the Farmhouse on Boone official recipe—I've added my Mama Fern tips for family kitchens.

## **1. Activate Your Starter (4-12 Hours Before)**

Feed your starter equal parts flour and water until it's **active, bubbly, and near peak**. This wild yeast culture is the heart of sourdough—patient and alive. Pro tip: If you're new, name it (mine's "Fernie" short for Fernandough the Starter) to make maintenance fun for kids (or childish adults). I don't measure feeds but make sure it looks proportionate to the amount of existing starter. 1 cup of starter means I'd feed it roughly 1 cup of flour and appropriate amount of room temperature water to get the consistency after mixed to be similar to that of pancake batter. If I'm using soon and want it active, it stays on the counter. If I don't have plans yet or need to slow it down, into the fridge it goes!

## **2. Mix the Dough (5 Mins Hands-On)**

In a large bowl, combine **water, active starter (100g), salt (10g), and flour (475g)**. Use your hands to mix for \~5 minutes until shaggy—no dry bits. Cover with a damp 100% cotton tea towel. Let **autolyse** (rest) for 1 hour. This hydrates the bran-heavy whole wheat, easing gluten development.​

## **3. Stretch and Folds (Gentle Building, 45-90 Mins)**

Skip kneading—**stretch-and-fold** preserves the dough's tenderness:

* **First 3 rounds**: Every 15 mins. Grab one side, stretch up 6 inches, fold over center. Rotate bowl 1/4 turn, repeat 3-4 times per round.
* **Last 3 rounds**: Every 30 mins. Same motion.

Your dough transforms from sticky to smooth, elastic silk.​ This is **not** a strict thing! Skip a couple, add more in, switch around the timing- it will still likely turn into delicious bread!

## **4. Bulk Fermentation (8 Hours)**

Cover with beeswax wrap.. Let rise in a warm spot until **doubled** (\~8 hours for me on the counter, I speed it up this time of year by placing near the fireplace). Look for bubbles and a domed top. Whole wheat ferments faster due to enzymes, so check on it every few hours.​

## **5. Shape the Boule (15-20 Mins Rest)**

On a counter, **spin dough into a tight ball** for tension—rotate toward you gently. Rest uncovered 15-20 mins (builds skin to prevent sticking). Flip onto floured surface, reshape tautly. Place seam-up in a **lightly floured banneton or tea towel-lined bowl**.​

## **6. Cold Proof (12-14 Hours Overnight)**

Cover with plastic (or bag it). Refrigerate 12-14 hours—or up to a week! This slow proof deepens flavor and fits busy schedules. Room-temp alternative: 2-4 hours warm. I usually do a double batch so ones put to bake and one in the fridge for long fermentation for another days quick meal.

## **7. Bake in Dutch Oven (40 Mins)**

* Preheat oven + Dutch oven to **500°F**.
* Remove dough from fridge, place on **parchment** (I opt to skip parchment since my cast iron is very well seasoned over the years). Score top (lame or razor—try leaves or crosses for faith-inspired designs).
* Dust flour atop (optional, highlights your design). Lift into hot pot, lid on.
* Bake **20 mins lidded**, **20 mins lidded off** at 500°F. Crust: deep mahogany. Internal: \~205°F. Cool fully (2+ hours) before slicing—patience sets the crumb.​ This timing is TRICKY for us- current oven is 20 minutes lid on and 5 off just to brown the crust. Keep an eye and nose out the first few times until you get to know your oven.

Result: A rustic boule with chewy crust, open(ish) crumb, and wheaty heart. Not store-fluffy (whole grains!), but superior in taste.​

## **Why Whole Wheat Sourdough Is Worth Learning**

This isn't hobby baking—it's **lifestyle nutrition**. Here's the payoff.

## **Health Edge: Gut-Friendly Fermentation**

Sourdough's lactic acid bacteria **predigest gluten and phytic acid**, boosting mineral absorption (iron, zinc) and easing digestion—vital for kids' tummies or sensitive adults. Whole wheat adds fiber (4g/slice), stabilizing blood sugar vs. white loaves. Studies back lower glycemic loads; I notice steadier energy.​​

## **Ease: Fits Real Life**

**\~50 mins active time** over 2 days. No daily kneading—just folds and waits. Fridge-proofing means bake-fresh bread anytime. Starter? Feed weekly; discards fuel pancakes or other low-rise goods. Forgiving for beginners—overproof? Understretch? Still edible.​

## **Flavor & Versatility**

Nutty, tangy depth from whole wheat + long ferment. Toast for breakfast, sandwich picnic lunches, or cube for stuffing. Customize: Add seeds, honey, or olives.​​ Didn't eat fast enough (unlikely but it happens!)- turn into bread crumbs!

## **Sustainability for Mama Fern Families**

Organic whole wheat aligns with our **chemical-free ethos**. Less packaging, zero preservatives. Teach kids fermentation as "kitchen alchemy"—ties to faith's themes of growth, patience.​

## **Ritual & Joy**

Baking slows chaos: Morning feed as prayer, evening folds as unwind. Share loaves with neighbors—builds community like our brand does. Visually? Crumb shots for Instagram gold.​

## **Bake Notes & Troubleshooting**

* **Flour**: Fresh-milled shines; sub store whole wheat.
* **Fluffier?** Try no-knead white sourdough next.
* **Dense crumb?** More hydration or warmer ferment.
* **Sticks?** Extra flour in banneton.
* **Store**: Room temp 3 days; freeze slices.​
